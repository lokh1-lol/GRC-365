Giveon - The Soulful Voice

Giveon Dezmann Evans, known simply as Giveon, is an American R&B singer-songwriter known for his distinctive baritone voice and emotionally rich lyrics.

Born on February 21, 1995, in Long Beach, California, Giveon first gained widespread recognition in 2020 when he was featured on Drake's song "Chicago Freestyle." His deep, soulful voice immediately captivated listeners around the world.

Giveon's music often explores themes of love, heartbreak, and vulnerability. His breakthrough EP "Take Time" was released in March 2020, followed by "When It's All Said and Done" in October of the same year.

In 2021, Giveon earned his first Grammy nomination for Best R&B Album for his debut studio album "Give or Take." His hit single "Heartbreak Anniversary" became a viral sensation on TikTok and streaming platforms, solidifying his place in contemporary R&B.

What sets Giveon apart is his ability to convey deep emotion through his rich baritone voice, often drawing comparisons to legendary singers like Barry White. His music combines modern R&B production with classic soul influences.

Despite his rapid rise to fame, Giveon maintains a relatively private personal life, letting his music speak for itself. He continues to be one of the most exciting and respected voices in modern R&B.
